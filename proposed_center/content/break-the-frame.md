# BREAK THE FRAME

## Designing What Doesn’t Yet Exist

## **In a world obsessed with efficiency, Break the Frame is a creative pause button. It’s a playshop designed for curious minds who are ready to unlearn, rethink, and rebuild. Here, we don’t just talk about innovation – we practice it through story, design, and experimentation.**

## What Happens in the Playshop

Think of it as a creative sprint where ideas get permission to breathe, bend, and break. Through hands-on exercises, storytelling, and rapid prototyping, participants move from “What is” to “What if.”

## Why It’s Different

### Break the Frame isn’t a workshop filled with slides and theory.

### It’s a space where design, strategy, and storytelling collide, where people rediscover how to imagine.

### Every idea needs a story that makes others believe in it.

# Meet the Instructor

**Lyle H. Sandler** is a designer, storyteller, and anthropologist who helps organizations reimagine how people connect with ideas, data, and environments. With a background in theatre, film, and design from Hofstra University and the American Film Institute, he has led creative transformation at global firms including Aon Corporation and NCR, weaving narrative thinking into strategic design.

An author and educator, Lyle’s recent book Universal Principles of Storytelling for Designers (Rockport, 2025) explores how storytelling can inspire meaningful design and innovation. His past works and practice continue to blur the lines between art, business, and human behavior.

Learn more at [lylesandler.com](https://www.lylesandler.com/)
